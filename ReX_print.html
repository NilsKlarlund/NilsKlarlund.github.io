ReX—towards a foundation for multimodal, multimedia applications

It is a fascinating fact that automated, interactive telephone systems, often called IVR-systems (IVR=Interactive Voice Response), remain frustratingly difficult to use. In large part, the reason is that their programming is so difficult. So here is an area where programming language research must be of importance? Unfortunately, no convincing solution ever emerged, and now something an order of magnitude more complicated is becoming commonplace: interactive, multimedia content!

Fortunately, interesting proposals have been formulated. For example, the W3C has proposed SMIL, a declarative notation for synchronizing media streams. But on the input side, which may include handwriting recognition and speech recognition on small devices, progress has been limited. Notations like VoiceXML lack a solid programming language foundation, and also do not take into account the fickleness of speech input. In particular, programmers must manually construct support for error correction.

We have tried to address these issues in our work on ReX, a reactive language for interactive, multimedia presentations. You can see our ideas in our paper.

By Nils Klarlund.
